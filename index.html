📘 How I Write User Stories

🎯 User Story (Concept-First)

Feature: [What are we building?]  
Summary: [One sentence that captures the user’s need or problem]  
Why It Matters: [A sentence or two connecting this feature to business or user goals]

Key Points:
- Behavior or rule 1
- Behavior or rule 2
- Edge cases or considerations

Acceptance Guidelines:
- [ ] Simple checkbox-style rules for testing or validation
- [ ] Designed to be skimmable, not painful

My stories are crafted to be developer-friendly: skimmable, clear, and paired with visuals and convos — not just Jira tickets.

📍 Example: Digital Patient Intake Form

  • Summary: Secure, mobile-friendly pre-appointment form
  • Why: Reduces burden and syncs with EHR
  • Key: Auto-save, real-time sync, triggered on scheduling
  • Acceptance: SMS link, 7-day trigger, form saves to EHR

📍 Example: Placement Denied by Vendor

  • Summary: Tracks denied requests with full details
  • Why: Enables insight for vendors & case managers
  • Tech: SQL + stored procs from HHAX std/enterprise
  • Acceptance: Accurate filters, export options, secure access
